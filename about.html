About Stanford

Our History

Stanford University was founded in 1885 by California senator Leland Stanford and his wife, Jane, in memory of their son, Leland Stanford Jr., who died of typhoid fever at age 15. The Stanfords decided to build a university of "absolute non-sectarianism and complete equality of opportunity regardless of gender, on their farm in California's Santa Clara Valley.

The university opened its doors in 1891, and from the outset was coeducational and non-denominational. The institution's first students enrolled in 1891, and the first bachelor's degrees were conferred in 1892.

In the early 20th century, Stanford struggled with financial challenges but persevered. The 1940s saw a dramatic increase in federal funding for scientific research, establishing Stanford as a leading research institution. The post-war period marked significant expansion, particularly in the sciences and engineering fields.

Today, Stanford is one of the world's leading research and teaching institutions, committed to finding solutions to challenges and to preparing students for leadership in today's complex world.

Campus and Location

Stanford's campus spans 8,180 acres in the heart of California's Silicon Valley, one of the most dynamic intellectual and entrepreneurial environments in the world. The main campus is remarkably beautiful, featuring distinctive sandstone buildings with red-tiled roofs, expansive open spaces, and a variety of sculpture gardens and art installations.

The university's location near San Francisco, San Jose, and the greater Bay Area provides students and faculty with a wealth of cultural, recreational, and professional opportunities. The region's innovative spirit infuses Stanford's culture of creativity and entrepreneurship.

By the Numbers

Students

Stanford's student body includes over 7,000 undergraduates and 9,000 graduate students from all 50 states and more than 90 countries.

The university admits students of exceptional academic ability without regard to their financial circumstances and provides significant financial aid to ensure all admitted students can attend.

Faculty

Stanford has approximately 2,300 faculty members and more than 1,900 postdoctoral scholars. Among the faculty are 20 Nobel laureates, 5 Pulitzer Prize winners, and 28 MacArthur Fellows.

Faculty members are leaders in their fields and are committed to both world-class research and exceptional teaching.
